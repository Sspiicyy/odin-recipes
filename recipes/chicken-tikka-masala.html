Chicken Tikka Masala

"This yummy dish will have you coming back for seconds!"

I've had some experience making curry, this one looks good. Here's the original, please check it out.

  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground tumeric
  • 1 14 ounce can of tomato sauce
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 16 ounces chicken thighs, bite sized
  • ½ teaspoon curry powder

Personally, I recommend a tub of unflavored plain yogurt as well. If you opt for this, double the spices, but separate the doubled amounts.

  1. If you opted to get yogurt, dice the chicken thighs, mix the chicken and all the spices into the yogurt. Let this sit in the fridge, ideally overnight.
  2. Gather all ingredients.
  3. Heat ghee in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in garlic; cook and stir just until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir cumin, 1 teaspoon salt, ginger, cayenne pepper, cinnamon, and turmeric into onion mixture; fry until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  5. Stir tomato sauce into onion and spice mixture; bring to a boil and reduce heat to low.
  6. Simmer sauce for 10 minutes, then mix in cream, 1 tablespoon sugar, and paprika. Bring sauce back to a simmer and cook, stirring often, until sauce is thickened, 10 to 15 minutes.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a separate skillet over medium heat. Stir chicken into hot oil; add curry powder (unless you opted for yogurt). Sear chicken until lightly browned but still pink inside, about 3 minutes; stir often.
  8. Transfer chicken and any pan juices into sauce. Simmer chicken in sauce until no longer pink, about 30 minutes; adjust sugar and salt to taste.
